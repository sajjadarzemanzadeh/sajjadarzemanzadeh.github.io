About

I am a Research Assistant in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Tehran. I received my bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with a GPA of 16.62/20 (last two years GPA: 17.52/20) from University of Tehran, and I was fortunate to be advised by Dr. Ehsan Hosseinian. During my undergraduate study, my main focus was on compound effect of shear and squeeze-film dissipation in a silicon lateral micro-resonator for MEMS-based environmental monitoring applications. Currently, I am a last-year master's student majoring in Biomechanics with a GPA of 17.91/20, and I am thrilled to work with Dr. Ehsan Hosseinian and Dr. Maryam Mahnama, and be a part of Multiscale Simulation of Materials and Structures (MSMS) Lab for the past two year. For my thesis, I am working on multiscale modeling of particle reinforced PNIPAM hydrogel to create tissue-like nanocomposite. I am interested in nanocomputational mechanics and multiscale modeling, molecular dynamics, MEMS and BioMEMS devices, biomimetic materials, nanocomposites, machine learning, and optimization.

Nonlinear System Identification of Soft Robot Dynamics

Mar. 2021 – July 2021

Instructor: Dr. MRH. Yazdi

The dynamic of a soft robotic actuatoe was estimated using data-driven methods [data source]. Due to nonlinearities in the properties of the soft materials, we cannot use the common linear system identification methods. Koopman operator theory is based on the fact that every nonlinear system has a linear reoresentation in the infinite-dimensional space. Koopman operator algorithm was applied on the data of a soft robotic arm, where the inputs of the model are the control input of three pressure regulators, and output is the end effector position. Besides, LSTM neural network was also utilized to model the soft robot. Time series of input voltage were given to the model to estimate the position of end effector. Trained LSTM model was imported into simulink environment to design a NN controller.
